How the Cubs broke the 108-year drought in 2016

2022-04-21

108 years.

That’s how long it had been since the Chicago Cubs won their last MLB World Series. After over a century, the “Lovable losers” knocked off the Cleveland Indians to take the series 4-3 and hoist the ultimate prize.

But how did they do it?

Sure they had the NL MVP and Cy Young in Kris Bryant and Jake Arrieta. But baseball is a team sport, so let’s take a deeper dive into the Cubs championship run.

Even though the Cubs knew how to get on base, an area of the game that doesn’t get talked about that much may have won them a World Series.

Defense.

Their fielding was on a different level in the 2016 season, as they saved nearly 40 more runs than the next closest team because of their play.

The Cubs defensive efficiency was through the roof, and they knew how to limit scores and make opponents grind for every singles run.

How could one forget about pitching.

The Cubs may have had one of the best pitching staffs in organization history.

Anchored by a staff of Kyle Hendricks, Jon Lester, John Lackey, and Arrieta, Chicago performed at an elite level on the mound.

Multiple players ranked in the top 10 for some of the most important pitching categories, namely earned run average and opponent batting average.

They kept the opposition guessing all season en route to a dramatic World Series victory.

It’s been long overdue for the city of Chicago to get a World Series trophy, and the 2016 season will go down as one of the most notable MLB teams ever.

They ended the curse of Steve Bartman and the 108-year long drought. The Cubs did it through stellar defense and an elite pitching staff. Alos, their NL MVP and Cy Young.

Hopefully it won’t be 108 more years till the next banner hangs at Wrigley Field.
